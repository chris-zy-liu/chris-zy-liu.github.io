title: Moon & Ceramic Shih Tzu in Pieces
layout: post
categories: Poetry
---
>Time is the moon<br>In wane and wax the portrait pages<br>Of a yellow almanac turns and stops<br>And so fortune was set<br>In bold crimson text.<br><br>(Molded plaster walls<br>In neglect grinds to flour)<br><br>Time is ceramic<br>In fingers and coins the reaper<br>Of war and plague comes and goes<br>And so fortune was set<br>In a mad mother's cry.<br><br>(Shallow roots of growth<br>In dinners feed a nation)<br><br>\*<br><br>Time is weight. Time is the silent stare of their eyes in the mirror.<br>Time is the curve on my back (the bend in my legs). Time is my empty hands<br>that I resigned to those who made me (those that fed). Time is the<br>Book of Songs. Time is Prometheus. Time is smiles without teeth.<br>Time is typhoon. Time is judgment in a little more than twelve hours.<br>Time is tragedy: a burning genius. Time is (in your heart of hearts) the<br>bends and squeezes of a maze long since travelled. Time is weight.<br>Time is (at the end of the universe) a solemn, lasting picnic. Across<br>the fourth filament Will and Fate sits directionless with Yin and Yang & at<br>the hours of my birth and death they made the moon. They made ceramic.
